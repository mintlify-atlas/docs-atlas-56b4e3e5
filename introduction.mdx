Maeil Mail (매일메일) was a subscription email service for software engineers preparing for technical interviews at Korean tech companies. Every day, subscribers received one carefully written question-and-answer pair covering a real interview topic — from JavaScript closures and React rendering, to JPA transactions and distributed system design. The service ran until its closure, at which point the entire content library was published as an open archive so engineers could continue learning from it.

## The service

Maeil Mail delivered questions across two tracks: frontend and backend. Each question was written to match the depth and framing used in actual technical interviews, with answers that went beyond surface-level definitions. Rather than a list of facts to memorize, each answer was structured to help you explain a concept clearly under interview conditions — the same skill that separates candidates who know something from those who can communicate it well.

The daily cadence was intentional. Rather than overwhelming subscribers with a study dump, one question per day created a sustainable habit. Over the course of months, a subscriber who engaged consistently would cover the full breadth of topics that come up in Korean software engineering interviews.

## Why this archive exists

When the Maeil Mail service ended, the question-and-answer content was too valuable to disappear. This repository preserves all of it in a browsable, searchable format — organized by topic rather than delivery date, so you can study systematically instead of sequentially.

The archive is open source and available on [GitHub](https://github.com/maeil-mail/maeil-mail-contents).
